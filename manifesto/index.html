Our Manifesto

Real work deserves real photos.

In a world where AI handles operations, we ensure visual storytelling stays human, providing authentic imagery that truly represents the incredible work being done by impact organizations worldwide.

Impact organizations are doing incredible work, but finding authentic, high-quality imagery that truly represents their mission is challenging and often expensive. Many are limited to generic stock photos, low-resolution images, or costly custom shoots that stretch budgets meant for program delivery.

Photography is an art form that deserves fair compensation, yet effective visual storytelling shouldn't be a luxury available only to well-funded organizations. Scatto bridges this gap by creating a sustainable model where photographers are paid fairly for their craft while keeping authentic imagery accessible to organizations of all sizes, from grassroots nonprofits to global foundations and mission-driven companies.

Quality visual storytelling should be accessible to every organization creating real change. As AI becomes the default solution for everything, we believe organizations making real impact deserve real storytelling through human eyes, captured by artists who understand the communities they photograph.

The eyes through which you tell your story determine how the world sees your impact. Authentic visuals don't just look better, they drive more donations, attract better partnerships, and build the trust that turns viewers into supporters.

We believe:

  • Authentic stories require authentic imagery
  • Photographers embedded in impact work see what others miss
  • Communities deserve control over how they're represented
  • Fair compensation builds sustainable storytelling

Our commitment:

  • Only photographers doing real impact work
  • Community consent, always
  • Photographer credit and fair revenue sharing
  • No AI, no exploitation, no generic diversity theater
The impact sector deserves better.
So do the communities it serves.

The Story Behind Scatto

After 7+ years working across the impact ecosystem, from international organizations to foundations, social ventures, and academia, I've seen this problem everywhere. Currently at the Paris-based office of J-PAL, a MIT-based impact evaluation center that aims to reduce poverty by ensuring policy is informed by scientific evidence, I've witnessed firsthand how the right visual story can amplify impact.

Years of struggling to find authentic, ethically-sourced imagery for my own work led me to build Scatto: a solution to connect organizations doing real change with photographers who understand their mission.

— Ottavia Brussino, Founder
